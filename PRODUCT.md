# Product

## Register

product

## Users

System admins, workflow admins, document admins, internal signers, auditors, and future external signers use the system to manage and sign SML-originated PDF documents. Most signing work happens on mobile or tablet devices, while admin and audit work happens mainly on desktop.

## Product Purpose

PaperLess V2 receives document metadata and PDFs, routes them through controlled signing workflows, records evidence, and prepares final signed documents for audit and later SML status sync. Success means users can sign the right document at the right step, admins can trace every action, and the system prevents duplicate, out-of-order, or unauthorized signing.

## Brand Personality

Calm, accountable, work-focused. The interface should feel like an operations tool people can trust during daily document approval work.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing landing-page layouts, decorative dashboards, playful signing flows, and shared-account assumptions. Avoid hiding errors behind generic messages. The product must not feel like a PDF toy; it must feel like a controlled business workflow.

## Design Principles

- Make identity and permission visible before any signing action.
- Keep admin surfaces dense enough for real work, but clear on status and next action.
- Treat mobile signing as a primary workflow, not a responsive afterthought.
- Preserve auditability in the interface language: who, what, when, and status.
- Start narrow and production-ready so workflow, PDF, and SML modules can be added safely.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, clear focus states, reduced-motion friendly transitions, and touch targets suitable for phones and tablets.
